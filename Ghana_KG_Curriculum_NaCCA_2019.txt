Ministry of Education, NaCCA — Kindergarten Curriculum (KG 1 & KG 2)
Core Framework Edition — 2019 Official Condensed Version

1. RATIONALE & PHILOSOPHY

Early childhood is the foundation of lifelong learning. The KG curriculum equips learners aged 4–5 with the skills, values, and dispositions for social participation and continuous education. It integrates cognitive, physical, social, emotional, moral, and creative development.
Learning is child-centred, play-based, inclusive, and contextualised in Ghanaian culture and global awareness.

2. GENERAL AIMS

Develop confident, creative, and curious learners.

Strengthen oral language, numeracy, and problem-solving.

Instil respect, cooperation, and cultural identity.

Build fine- and gross-motor coordination through play.

Encourage early digital awareness and curiosity about nature.

3. CORE COMPETENCIES

Critical Thinking & Problem-Solving – asking questions, reasoning, predicting.

Creativity & Innovation – expressing ideas through play, music, art.

Communication & Collaboration – speaking clearly, taking turns, sharing.

Cultural Identity & Global Citizenship – pride in Ghanaian heritage, openness to others.

Personal Development & Leadership – self-control, responsibility, empathy.

Digital Literacy – using age-appropriate technology safely.

4. LEARNING DOMAINS

Knowledge & Understanding: facts, ideas, symbols.

Application: using learning in real situations.

Attitudes & Values: curiosity, perseverance, honesty, teamwork.

5. PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH

Learning through play, exploration, songs, storytelling, projects, and guided discovery.
Teacher acts as facilitator; environment provides rich stimuli.
Key methods: learning centres, group work, outdoor activities, role-play, guided conversation, and ICT-assisted learning.

6. ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

Assessment supports learning through:

Observation: continuous monitoring of behaviour and performance.

Checklists & Portfolios: record individual progress.

Anecdotal Notes & Conversations: qualitative feedback.

Reporting: descriptive statements, not grades.
Weighting: 70 % formative | 30 % summative.

7. INTEGRATED LEARNING AREAS

Language & Literacy | Numeracy | Our World & Our People | Creative Arts
All domains overlap; each theme draws from several areas.

8. THEMATIC STRUCTURE
THEME 1: ALL ABOUT ME

Focus: identity, body awareness, hygiene, feelings, growth.

Recognise parts of the body and their functions.

Practise personal hygiene and safety habits.

Express emotions appropriately.

Demonstrate self-confidence through introductions and role-play.

Identify likes, dislikes, talents, and gender differences respectfully.

KG 2 Additions:

Explain how healthy habits affect growth.

Relate emotions to situations (happy, sad, afraid).

Discuss similarities/differences among peers.

THEME 2: MY FAMILY

Focus: family structure, love, respect, cooperation.

Identify members of the nuclear and extended family.

Describe roles of mother, father, siblings, elders.

Participate in chores and caring activities.

Practise greetings and courtesy expressions in home languages.

Appreciate diversity of family types.

KG 2 Additions:

Compare past and present family life.

Express gratitude and resolve conflicts peacefully.

THEME 3: VALUES AND BELIEFS

Focus: morality, honesty, religion, respect.

Retell simple moral stories.

Demonstrate honesty, kindness, sharing.

Recognise symbols of faith (cross, mosque, shrine).

Practise respect during prayer and cultural rituals.

Uphold national and community values.

KG 2 Additions:

Discuss consequences of right and wrong.

Participate in songs promoting unity and peace.

THEME 4: MY LOCAL COMMUNITY

Focus: environment, occupations, safety, cooperation.

Identify community helpers (farmer, nurse, teacher).

Describe simple tools used in local jobs.

Practise road safety and cleanliness.

Show respect for community property.

Observe rules at home and school.

KG 2 Additions:

Compare rural and urban settings.

Demonstrate teamwork in community games or projects.

THEME 5: MY NATION GHANA

Focus: patriotism, symbols, leadership, culture.

Identify national flag, anthem, and coat of arms.

Learn key national days and leaders.

Sing and recite patriotic songs and rhymes.

Appreciate major ethnic groups and languages.

Discuss peace and unity as citizens' duty.

KG 2 Additions:

Describe national resources and landmarks.

Recognise Ghana on a simple map of Africa.

THEME 6: ALL AROUND US

Focus: nature, plants, animals, seasons, weather.

Identify living and non-living things.

Observe growth of plants and care for them.

Classify domestic and wild animals.

Discuss effects of weather on daily life.

Demonstrate environmental care (do not litter, water plants).

KG 2 Additions:

Record simple weather observations.

Explain importance of water, air, and sunlight.

Participate in recycling and conservation projects.

THEME 7: MY GLOBAL COMMUNITY

Focus: wider world, technology, cooperation beyond borders.

Identify forms of transport and communication.

Recognise clothing, food, and music from other cultures.

Discuss similarities among children worldwide.

Use simple ICT tools (radio, camera, tablet) responsibly.

Appreciate peace and friendship among nations.

KG 2 Additions:

Locate Ghana's neighbours on a map.

Demonstrate curiosity about science and space.

Role-play global friendship and online communication (teacher-guided).

9. NUMERACY HIGHLIGHTS

Counting & Number Concepts: 0–50 (KG 1), 0–100 (KG 2).
Geometry & Measurement: shapes, size, weight, time, money.
Patterns & Problem Solving: sequencing, classification, estimation.
Methods: concrete–pictorial–abstract progression; songs, games, manipulatives.

10. CREATIVE ARTS & PHYSICAL DEVELOPMENT

Drawing, painting, modelling, dancing, singing, dramatization.

Encourage originality and cultural expression.

Develop balance, coordination, and rhythm through play.

Showcase work during class exhibitions or performances.

11. OUR WORLD AND OUR PEOPLE (SOCIAL & ENVIRONMENTAL LEARNING)

Awareness of community, culture, and environment.

Develop civic sense, empathy, and sustainability habits.

Explore basic science: plants, soil, water, animals.

12. LANGUAGE AND LITERACY

Listening and speaking through rhymes, storytelling, conversations.

Early reading via phonemic awareness, picture recognition.

Writing: tracing, copying, letter formation, short sentences.

Vocabulary drawn from daily life, environment, and culture.

Encourage bilingualism using both Ghanaian and English languages.

13. ICT IN LEARNING

Use age-appropriate devices for songs, pictures, counting.

Introduce safe handling of digital tools.

Integrate audio-visual aids to enhance curiosity and participation.

14. INCLUSION & SPECIAL NEEDS

Modify activities to accommodate pace and ability.

Use tactile materials, sign language, peer support, flexible grouping.

Promote acceptance and empathy within the class.

15. VALUES AND ATTITUDES IN ACTION

Respect, honesty, discipline, teamwork, responsibility.

Daily routines begin and end with moral reflection or prayer.

Reinforce values through stories, songs, and classroom roles.

16. TIME ALLOCATION

10 periods per week (30 min each). Suggested breakdown:

Language & Literacy – 3

Numeracy – 3

Our World & People – 2

Creative Arts – 1

Physical Development – 1

17. TEACHER GUIDELINES

Use continuous observation instead of tests.

Encourage expression through talk, drawing, acting.

Link classroom learning to home and community.

Celebrate effort, not only accuracy.

Keep anecdotal records of each child's growth.

18. EXPECTED LEARNER PROFILE AT END OF KG 2

A typical KG graduate should be able to:

Communicate confidently in English and local language.

Demonstrate curiosity and respect for others.

Count, sort, and describe shapes.

Express ideas through art, music, and movement.

Care for environment and practice hygiene.

Work cooperatively and show empathy.

19. APPENDIX — COMPETENCY MAPPING
Core Competency	Typical Learning Demonstration
Critical Thinking	Explains reasons for choices, predicts outcomes.
Creativity	Produces original art or stories.
Communication	Listens and responds appropriately.
Cultural Identity	Uses Ghanaian language respectfully.
Personal Development	Manages emotions, helps peers.
Digital Literacy	Operates digital tools under guidance.
